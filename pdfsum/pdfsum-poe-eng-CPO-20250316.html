CPO is Coming But Adoption Takes Time

Buy T&S/TFC Optical as Networking Winners in Channel Expansion

Citi Research Report March 11, 2025

Executive Summary

CITI'S TAKE

Although Nvidia may introduce CPO switches during its GTC conference, we expect CPO switch adoption to take time and gradually reach ~25-30% in 2028-29, thus parallel volume growth of transceiver names and CPO-related players in 2026-27 is likely. CPO Scale-up (OIO) for xPU interconnection remains a lucrative market for optics in the long term.

We forecast CPO switch shipment (including likely OIO trial in 2027) to reach 300/5k/100k in 2025/26/27E respectively. Within networking space, we see ELSFP/FAU & Connectors/Fiber Shuffle as key components inside while T&S would benefit from increasing fiber shuffle demand and TFC would enjoy ELSFP/FAU connector tailwinds in CPO/OIO.

We view both as key CPO winners as they would benefit from increasing number of channels ahead. We revise our estimates for T&S, TFC, Innolight and Eoptolink, while opening 30-day upside catalyst watches for T&S and TFC ahead of GTC. T&S is our sector top pick, followed by TFC.

CPO Launch & Timeline

We expect CPO solutions of Nvidia to start sample trials in 2025 while there will be 1-2 versions for both IB/Ethernet this year, one for InfiniBand and one for Ethernet (Spectrum X) while we may see CPO have its mass production model for Rubin next year.

Despite CPO likely launching soon, we believe CPO scale-out is paving way for OIO for xPU interconnection in 2027/28 to boost bandwidth density/power efficiency and it is a lucrative long-term TAM for optics.

Key Network Components

We view FAU (Fiber Array Units)/Connectors, ELSFP (External laser small form factor pluggable), Fiber Shuffle as key components of CPO/OIO, while we forecast the market size could reach Rmb3.0bn/10.7bn/3.3bn in 2027.

Initial content value of FAU/ELSFP/Fiber Shuffle could be US$100/US$1100/~US$7000-14000 (1 or 2 Fiber Shuffle Solutions) respectively. We like T&S / TFC due to their product strength and strong relationships with key customers.

CPO Technology Overview

What is CPO?

Co-Packaged Optics (CPO) integrates optical components directly with switch ASICs in the same package, reducing power consumption and increasing bandwidth density compared to traditional pluggable transceivers.

The transition away from pluggable transceivers to CPO is driven by increasing bandwidth requirements and power efficiency concerns in AI and data center networking.

Evolving Optics Layout

Key Components of CPO

FAU (Fiber Array Units)

Connects single mode fiber and OE (PIC) for optics coupling through the waveguide inside PIC. Expected ASP of ~US$100 at launch.

ELSFP (External Laser Small Form Factor Pluggable)

Houses continuous wave lasers to provide optical power to PIC. Allows easier thermal management by decoupling laser from ASIC. Initial pricing ~US$1,100.

Fiber Shuffle

Addresses inconsistent fiber length and cable routing challenges. Content value of ~US$7,000-14,000 based on 1 or 2 Fiber Shuffle Solutions.

OIO (Optical I/O) Potential

We see adoption of CPO technology over switch from leading GPU/ASIC players as paving the future path of OIO for xPU interconnection using relevant technology. Although this may come in for Rubin Ultra or even the next generation GPU and beyond, we still see OIO as a very lucrative market.

The increase in bandwidth will finally shorten transmission distance of copper, which is a physical limitation, and its signal quality will worsen. Thus NVLink Switch interconnection will finally migrate to optics from copper, when SerDes further increases and reaches a ceiling.

Each switch tray can be potentially placed with one fiber shuffle to help connect different GPUs inside. Assuming the current setup of 9 switches tray per NVL72 rack, if there is 4mn units of GPU in form of DGX NVL72 rack like solutions, there will be ~500k switch trays, versus ~139k IB Switches.
